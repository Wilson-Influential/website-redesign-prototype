TikTok ads: where should a challenger brand start?

Example post to demonstrate the new insight layout · 5 minute read

If you found this post through LinkedIn, you're in good company. When we post consistently there, more than half of the visits to our site in a month start with a post like this one. That taught us something about our own website: the article can't be the end of the road. So at the bottom of this page you'll find the service behind this thinking and the work that proves it, not just a full stop.

Start with the audience, not the platform

The first question isn't "should we be on TikTok?" It's "is the audience we need actually there, and what do they watch?" A week of proper audience research beats a month of guessing. If your buyers are there, the platform rewards brands that behave like creators rather than advertisers.

Creative is the targeting

On TikTok, the algorithm finds the audience your creative deserves. Three rough-cut variations of a native-feeling video will nearly always outperform one polished advert. Plan to make more, cheaper, faster, and let the results tell you which idea earns the budget.

Spend small, learn fast

A modest test budget, run properly for four weeks, answers the question a strategy document never can. Set one clear success measure before you start. If it works, scale it. If it doesn't, you've bought certainty at a low price.
